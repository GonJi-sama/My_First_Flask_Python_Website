Background and Importance

Sydney's background is rooted in its founding as a British penal colony in 1788 on the shores of Port Jackson, and its importance today lies in its status as Australia's largest and a leading global city. It is Australia's financial hub, a major center for international tourism, and a hub for culture and entertainment, boasting iconic landmarks like the Opera House and Harbour Bridge.

Indigenous History: Before European arrival, the land was inhabited for tens of thousands of years by the Gadigal people of the Eora Nation, whose cultural traditions are a deep part of Sydney's identity.

British Settlement: The modern history of Sydney began in 1788 when the First Fleet, a group of British convicts, established a penal colony at Sydney Cove.
Colonial Growth: The settlement quickly grew into a bustling port city due to its strategic location along Pacific trade routes, driven by agriculture and trade.

19th and 20th Century: Sydney continued to expand through waves of immigration and developed into a major center for commerce and government, surpassing Melbourne as the financial center in the 1970s.
